Problem Statement

Agricultural mechanization in hilly regions is currently hindered by a critical trade-off: farmers are forced to choose between portable but low-capacity mini threshers or high-capacity machines that are too heavy to transport across difficult terrain. This lack of suitable machinery perpetuates manual drudgery and limits productivity.

Hypothesis

By substituting conventional Mild Steel with lightweight Glass Fiber Reinforced Polymer (GFRP) and integrating a precisely sized DC motor with a Solar PV system, we can create a thresher that is both highly portable and sustainable—effectively bridging the gap for marginal hill farmers.

Thesis Objectives

  • Conducted static and dynamic structural analysis of threshing drum components using SolidWorks.
  • Substituted traditional steel with Glass Fiber Reinforced Polymer (GFRP) to reduce machine weight.
  • Designed a robust mechanical drive system powered by a 450W DC motor.
  • Developed a portable system specifically for the topographical challenges of marginal hill farmers.

Key Outcomes

  • Successfully threshed paddy at 14% moisture content with minimal grain damage.
  • Achieved peak solar generation of 198.72 W during field trials.
  • Slashed operational costs to ₹1,298/tonne compared to manual or diesel methods.
  • Received the Best Poster Presentation Award at the 58th ISAE Annual Convention (2024).
