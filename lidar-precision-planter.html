PhD Research Case Study

Integrated Mechatronic System for
Precision Turmeric Planting

Addressing singulation errors and ground-wheel skidding through active LIDAR velocity synchronization and three-stage progressive metering for precision stand establishment.

Field Assessment

ISSUE 01

Automation Scarcity

Current turmeric planting machinery is predominantly semi-automatic; fully automated solutions are often repurposed from other crops and lack the specialization required for turmeric rhizomes.

ISSUE 02

Singulation Complexity

Due to the irregular morphology of turmeric rhizomes, achieving precise singulation during metering is difficult, leading to multiple-seed drops and inflated seed rates.

ISSUE 03

Check-Row Discontinuity

Lack of drop-point control results in irregular crop spacing, making intercultural operations and mechanical weeding highly difficult.

ISSUE 04

Drive-Train Slippage

Traditional ground-wheel drives are prone to skidding and slippage, leading to significant errors in seed-to-seed spacing accuracy.

System Hypothesis & Solution

FIG 01

Morphological Optimization

Engineering metering cells specifically optimized for turmeric rhizome geometry to ensure stable physical handling.

FIG 02

Three-Stage Metering

Volume is reduced progressively across three tiered stages, each driven by independent motors, to isolate single rhizomes.

FIG 03

Active Speed Sync

Ground wheels replaced by a LIDAR-Encoder fusion system to drive stepper motors in real-time sync with tractor velocity.

FIG 04

Solenoid Delivery

A triggered delivery gate regulates the exact spatial drop-point, allowing for consistent check-row patterns.
